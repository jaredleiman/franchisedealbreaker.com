Is Orangetheory Fitness a Good Franchise to Buy?

Orangetheory Fitness is a boutique fitness franchise offering heart rate-based interval training with over 1,500 studios worldwide. Founded in 2010 in Fort Lauderdale, Florida, the brand popularized the science-backed 'orange zone' workout methodology.

Total investment to open an Orangetheory Fitness studio ranges from approximately $563,000 to $999,000 — one of the highest entry costs in the boutique fitness category. This reflects premium buildout requirements, specialized equipment, and technology infrastructure.

Key questions for prospective Orangetheory franchisees include the sustainability of boutique fitness demand post-pandemic, competition from Peloton and at-home fitness alternatives, and the high investment-to-earnings ratio relative to other fitness concepts.

How much does a Orangetheory Fitness franchise cost?

Total investment ranges from $563,000 to $999,000 with an initial franchise fee of $59,950. The 8% royalty on gross sales is among the higher rates in the fitness category. High buildout costs and equipment requirements make this one of the more capital-intensive fitness franchises.

Has Orangetheory Fitness been sued by franchisees?

Orangetheory has disclosed litigation in recent FDD filings. Some franchisee-initiated actions have been noted. Buyers should review the full Item 3 disclosures carefully in their specific FDD and discuss any open matters with a franchise attorney.

What does Orangetheory Fitness's Item 19 say about earnings?

Orangetheory's earnings disclosures have been limited relative to the investment required. Item 19 typically covers gross revenue for a subset of studios. Given the high capital requirement, buyers should seek detailed validation from existing franchisees and request unit-level economics.
